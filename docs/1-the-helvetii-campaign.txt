  Book 1: Helvetii Campaign — Actions, Locations & Actors

  Background (Ch. 2-5) — Helvetii homeland (Switzerland)

  1. Orgetorix's conspiracy — Helvetii territory. Orgetorix persuades the Helvetii to migrate, recruits Casticus (Sequani) and Dumnorix (Aedui) into
  a three-way power pact
  2. Orgetorix's trial & death — Helvetii territory. Orgetorix is tried, gathers 10,000 supporters, dies (possibly suicide)
  3. Helvetii burn everything — Helvetii territory. They burn 12 towns, ~400 villages, all excess grain. Rauraci, Tulingi, Latobrigi join them. Boii
  also join

  The Geneva Confrontation (Ch. 6-8) — Geneva / Lake Léman / Rhône

  4. Caesar rushes to Geneva — from Rome to Geneva. Caesar with one legion (Legio X, already in Transalpine Gaul). Orders the bridge at Geneva
  destroyed
  5. Helvetii embassy — Geneva. Numeius and Verudoctius ask permission to cross the Province. Caesar stalls, tells them to come back April 12
  6. Caesar builds the wall — 19 miles from Lake Geneva to Mt. Jura along the south bank of the Rhône. Caesar, Legio X, + provincial levies. 16-foot
  wall + trench with redoubts
  7. Caesar denies passage — Geneva. Ambassadors return, Caesar refuses
  8. Helvetii attempt to force crossing — Rhône (various fords and bridges of boats). Repelled by Roman fortifications

  The Sequani Detour (Ch. 9) — Jura pass / Sequani territory

  9. Dumnorix brokers passage — Sequani territory. Dumnorix persuades the Sequani to let the Helvetii pass through the narrow Jura route. Mutual
  hostages exchanged

  Caesar Gathers His Army (Ch. 10) — Italy → Alps → Transalpine Gaul

  10. Caesar leaves Labienus at Geneva — Geneva fortifications. Titus Labienus left in command of the wall
  11. Caesar marches to Italy — Forced marches to Cisalpine Gaul. Levies 2 new legions, collects 3 more from Aquileia winter quarters (= 5 legions
  total)
  12. Caesar crosses the Alps — Alpine passes. Centrones, Graioceli, Caturiges try to block him; Caesar defeats them in several battles
  13. Caesar reaches Vocontii territory — Further Province (7 days from Ocelum). Then through Allobroges territory to the Segusiavi (first people
  beyond the Province, across the Rhône)

  The Pursuit (Ch. 11-15) — Aedui territory / Saône River

  14. Helvetii ravage Aedui lands — Aedui territory. Aedui, Ambarri, Allobroges all send embassies to Caesar begging help
  15. Battle at the Saône — Saône River (in Aedui/Sequani territory). Caesar attacks the Tigurine canton (the last quarter still crossing). Most
  killed, rest flee to the woods
  16. Caesar bridges the Saône — Saône River. Crosses in one day (took the Helvetii 20 days)
  17. Embassy of Divico — near the Saône. Divico (Helvetii commander from the old Cassius defeat) demands peace or threatens war. Caesar demands
  hostages and reparations to the Aedui. Negotiations fail
  18. Roman cavalry skirmish — on the march. Caesar's 4,000 cavalry (from Province + Aedui) pursue too eagerly, are repulsed by 500 Helvetii horse.
  Dumnorix and his Aedui cavalry blamed for starting the rout

  The Corn Crisis & Dumnorix Affair (Ch. 16-20) — on the march, Aedui territory

  19. Caesar demands corn from Aedui — camp, on the march. Divitiacus and Liscus (Vergobretus) summoned. Liscus reveals sabotage
  20. Dumnorix exposed — Caesar's camp. Liscus names Dumnorix as the saboteur — arranged Helvetii passage, commanded the cavalry rout, married to
  Orgetorix's daughter
  21. Caesar confronts Dumnorix — Caesar's camp. Divitiacus tearfully begs for his brother. Caesar pardons Dumnorix but sets spies on him

  The Aborted Ambush (Ch. 21-22) — near Bibracte

  22. Night march plan — near the Helvetii camp, a mountain 8 miles away. Caesar sends Labienus with 2 legions to take the summit by night. Publius
  Considius sent ahead as scout
  23. Considius's false report — near the mountain. Considius panics, reports (falsely) that the enemy holds the summit. Caesar withdraws. Labienus
  holds position but doesn't engage (per orders)

  Battle of Bibracte (Ch. 23-26) — near Bibracte (Aedui capital)

  24. Caesar diverts to Bibracte for supplies — heading toward Bibracte, 18 miles away. Deserters (from Lucius Aemilius, Gallic cavalry officer) tell
   the Helvetii
  25. Helvetii pursue and attack — a hill near Bibracte. Helvetii reverse course and hit Caesar's rear
  26. Main battle — hillside near Bibracte. Caesar deploys 4 veteran legions on the slope, 2 new legions + auxiliaries on the summit. Helvetii form
  phalanx, charge uphill, are broken by javelin volley and sword charge
  27. Boii/Tulingi flank attack — same battlefield. 15,000 Boii and Tulingi hit the Roman flank. Helvetii rally. Romans split into two divisions.
  Fighting lasts from noon to nightfall, then into the night at the wagon laager

  Surrender & Settlement (Ch. 27-29) — Lingones territory → back to Helvetii homeland

  28. Helvetii flee — from Bibracte to Lingones territory (4 days' march). ~130,000 survivors. Caesar warns the Lingones not to help them
  29. Helvetii surrender — on the road in Lingones territory. Deliver hostages, arms, deserters. 6,000 Verbigeni try to escape to the Rhine; are
  hunted down
  30. Caesar orders them home — Helvetii/Tulingi/Latobrigi ordered back to their burned homeland. Allobroges ordered to supply them corn. Boii
  settled in Aedui territory at Aedui request
  31. Census — Helvetii camp. Greek-character lists found: 368,000 departed, 110,000 returned

  ---
  Key people:

  - Caesar — present from Ch. 7 onward
  - Labienus — left at Geneva wall (Ch. 10), leads night flanking force (Ch. 21-22)
  - Dumnorix (Aedui) — villain throughout, brokers Helvetii passage, sabotages corn supply, causes cavalry rout
  - Divitiacus (Aedui) — Caesar's ally, Dumnorix's brother, pleads for mercy
  - Divico — Helvetii general, leads the failed peace embassy
  - Considius — scout who panics and ruins the ambush
  - Orgetorix — instigator, dies before the migration begins

  Key locations in sequence:

  Geneva → Rhône wall → (Jura pass for Helvetii) → Cisalpine Gaul/Aquileia → Alps → Allobroges → Saône River → Aedui territory → near Bibracte →
  Lingones territory
