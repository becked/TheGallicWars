Senior command and political figures ￼

The core high‑command cast:

- Gaius Iulius Caesar – Proconsul and narrator, commander-in-chief throughout.

- Titus Labienus – Legatus pro praetore, Caesar’s chief deputy in Gaul, present and named in multiple books (I–VII).

- Marcus Licinius Crassus – Caesar’s quaestor in Gaul (V–VI).

- Publius Licinius Crassus – His son, often in independent command (I–III).

- Decimus Brutus – Commands the fleet against the Veneti (III, VII).

- Lucius Caesar – A legate under Caesar (VII).

- Marcus Antonius (Mark Antony) – Appears as a legatus in Book VII.

- Quintus Tullius Cicero – Caesar’s officer (effectively a legate) in Belgic campaigns and the famous winter-camp siege (V–VII).

Legates and high-ranking officers ￼

Named Roman legates and comparable senior officers:

- Quintus Titurius Sabinus – Legatus; co‑commands a legion that is destroyed after he trusts Ambiorix (II–VI).

- Lucius Aurunculeius Cotta – Legatus; shares that doomed command with Sabinus and is killed (II, IV–VI).

- Quintus Pedius – Legatus; Caesar’s nephew (II).

- Servius Galba – Legatus in the Alps (III).

- Gaius Fabius – Legatus in several campaigns (V–VII).

- Lucius Munatius Plancus – Legatus (V).

- Gaius Trebonius – First as a Roman knight, later as legatus (V–VII).

- Gaius Antistius Reginus – Legatus (VI–VII).

- Gaius Caninius Rebilus – Legatus (VII).

- Titus Sextius – Legatus (VI–VII).

- Marcus Silanus (Brutus Silanus) – Legatus (VI).

- Publius Sulpicius Rufus – Legatus (IV, VII).

- Lucius Roscius – Legion commander in winter quarters (V).

Tribunes and staff officers ￼

Named tribunes and staff, often captured or used as messengers:

- Marcus Trebius Gallus – Tribune, captured by coastal Gauls (III).

- Titus Silius – Tribune, captured by coastal Gauls (III).

- Quintus Velanius – Tribune, captured by coastal Gauls (III).

- Quintus Volusenus – Tribune who scouts the coast of Britain before Caesar’s first crossing (IV).

Centurions and battlefield “heroes” ￼

Roman centurions Caesar singles out:

- Publius Sextius Baculus – Veteran chief centurion; key figure at Aduatuca and in the Alpine campaign (II, III, VI).

- Titus Balventius – Chief centurion killed in Ambiorix’s ambush (V).

- Lucanius – Chief centurion also killed in Ambiorix’s trap (V).

- Lucius Petrosidius – Standard-bearer who throws the eagle inside the camp in the Sabinus/Cotta disaster (V).

- Lucius Fabius – Centurion killed after climbing the wall at Gergovia (VII).

- Titus Pullo – Centurion and rival of Vorenus in the famous episode in Cicero’s camp (V.44).

- Lucius Vorenus – Centurion, Pullo’s rival and co‑hero of that same story (V.44).

Roman envoys, notables, and other individuals ￼

Other named Romans acting as envoys, local chiefs with Roman citizenship, or notable subordinates:

- Gaius Valerius Procillus – Gallic-born Roman citizen used as Caesar’s envoy to Ariovistus, captured and dramatically rescued (I).

- Marcus Mettius – Envoy sent with Procillus to Ariovistus, also taken prisoner (I).

- Gaius Valerius Donnotaurus – Chief of the Helvii, with Roman tria nomina; killed by rebels (VII).

- Vertico’s slave is mentioned but unnamed; Vertico himself is a Nervian, so non‑Roman.
